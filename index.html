Thinking

Philosophy

How I think about talent, hiring, and building functions that actually last.

These are not frameworks I read about. They are conclusions I reached by doing the work, making mistakes, and paying close attention to what actually moved the needle.

01
Hiring is a system, not a series of transactions.
Every role you fill should make the next one easier. That only happens when you build infrastructure — pipelines, process, data, playbooks — that compounds over time. I do not just fill seats. I build the engine that keeps filling them. People plus data plus the right tools, working together.
02
Technology should augment the human parts, not replace them.
AI and automation should make recruiters faster, sharper, and better informed — not remove them from the equation. The empathy, the read on a candidate, the relationship with a hiring manager — those stay human. A world-class talent function is heavily augmented by technology. It is never fully reliant on it.
03
Data tells you where to go. Judgment gets you there.
I run experiments. I track signal. I double down on what works and cut what does not. But data does not make the final call — it informs it. Knowing how to read a number and knowing what it means for a human being are two different skills. You need both to build something that lasts.
04
Stakeholder trust is the job.
A talent function is only as strong as its relationship with the people it serves. Founders, CTOs, VPs, hiring managers — if they do not trust the process, they bypass it. My job is to earn that trust early, hold the standard when it is uncomfortable, and make hiring feel like a strategic advantage rather than a bottleneck.
